<p><p>The desalination facility market comprises several technologies used to convert seawater or brackish water into freshwater. Reverse Osmosis (RO) utilizes semi-permeable membranes to remove salts and impurities, making it energy-efficient and widely adopted. Multi-Stage Flash (MSF) involves heating seawater to create steam, which is then condensed to produce freshwater, suitable for large-scale operations. Other methods include Multi-Effect Distillation (MED) and Electrodialysis, offering alternatives based on specific water quality, energy requirements, and regional needs, facilitating diverse applications in water-scarce areas.</p></p>
